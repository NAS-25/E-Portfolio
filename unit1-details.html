Security and Risk Management

This unit explores the foundational concepts of Risk, Security, and Risk Management (SRM), which are essential in understanding how organisations protect their assets and information. Risk is broadly defined as the likelihood of a threat exploiting a vulnerability, leading to loss or damage. It involves assessing both the probability of occurrence and the potential impact. The unit emphasises the distinction between threats, which are potential causes of unwanted incidents (such as cyberattacks or natural disasters), and vulnerabilities, which are weaknesses that can be exploited by these threats.

Students are introduced to the Risk Management Process (RMP), a structured approach that includes identifying, assessing, mitigating, monitoring, and reviewing risks. The goal of the RMP is to reduce risks to an acceptable level through appropriate controls and strategies. Furthermore, the unit reviews major risk management standards and frameworks, such as ISO 31000, NIST SP 800-30, and ISO/IEC 27005, which provide systematic methods for managing information security risks.

By studying these frameworks, learners gain an understanding of best practices for ensuring security and resilience within organisations. Overall, this unit builds a solid foundation for analysing and managing security risks in dynamic and complex environments.

Cybersecurity Overview

The MSc Cybersecurity programme provides a comprehensive exploration of the principles, technologies, and practices that underpin the protection of digital systems and data in an increasingly interconnected world. It equips learners with both theoretical understanding and hands-on experience in defending networks, managing security risks, and responding to evolving cyber threats. The MSc Cybersecurity journey not only strengthens technical expertise but also emphasises strategic and ethical considerations—preparing graduates to contribute effectively to the design and governance of secure information systems.
